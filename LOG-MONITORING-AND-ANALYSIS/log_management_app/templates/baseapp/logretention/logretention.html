Overview of Log Retention Policies

Log retention policies are essential for managing the lifecycle of your logs, ensuring that they are stored for the required period and then appropriately archived or deleted. Effective policies help in compliance with legal and regulatory requirements and optimize storage resources.

Key Benefits:

  • Compliance with industry regulations and standards.
  • Efficient use of storage resources.
  • Improved performance by removing outdated logs.
Retention Policies for Log Sources
System Logs:

Windows Logs: Retain for 90 days.

Linux Logs: Retain for 60 days.

MacOS Logs: Retain for 30 days.

Application Logs:

Webserver Logs: Retain for 180 days.

Database Logs: Retain for 120 days.

Middleware Caching Logs: Retain for 90 days.
